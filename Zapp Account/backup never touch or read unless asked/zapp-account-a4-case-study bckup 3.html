Building Zapp Account

Rebrand and upgrade the existing digital wallet for PayzApp into a digital bank account that could do it all.

Role: Product Designer Responsibility: Strategy, Team Lead, Design

What is Zapp Account?

Zapp Account is a digital prepaid payments account offered through PayZapp, built to give users a more capable, controlled, and convenient way to pay, transact, and manage everyday money. It supports UPI, Tap and Pay, Peer to Peer, and Online Payments.

What was it before and What the bank wanted

Before Zapp Account, the product existed as PayzApp Wallet: a digital wallet inside PayzApp that supported recharges, bill payments, and a virtual card for tap-and-pay and online transactions. It had some adoption inside the app, but not the kind of traction that made it feel like a central financial product. The bank wanted to bring renewed focus to this wallet. Their marketing team suggested the name Zapp Account, so the wallet could sit better inside a larger product ecosystem and grow beyond a wallet-like mental model.

We needed to get following things done:

01 / Introduce Video KYC To Unlock Full Capabilities

The most immediate ask was Video KYC. Until then, non-bank customers did not have a clear path to the full capabilities of Zapp Account. Bank customers could complete 4D card verification and unlock the complete experience, but everyone else was limited. Video KYC changed that: it gave non-bank users a route to full access and made the product more open to the wider market.

02 / Rebrand PayZapp Wallet to Zapp Account Across inside and outside the App

This initiative covered the comprehensive rebranding of PayZapp Wallet to Zapp Account within the app. The scope includes updating all icons, nomenclature, and user nudges previously used for PayZapp Wallet education and promotion. Additionally, because the wallet is integrated externally via the "Pay with PayZapp" feature, the rebranding would extend across all third-party merchant domains.

What we wanted to achieve

Increase wallet loads, improve adoption, and elevate the overall experience.

We were focusing on three core outcomes for Zapp Account: getting more users to enroll, increasing the amount they loaded into the wallet, and improving the experience enough for the product to feel easier to understand and use.

01 / Improve Adoption

Adoption meant getting more people to enroll for Zapp Account in the first place. This became especially important because a large share of PayZapp users were new-to-bank or non-bank customers. With Video KYC, those users could now become eligible for the full Zapp Account experience. The opportunity was no longer limited to the existing bank customer base; we had to make the value proposition clear enough for this wider audience to sign up: speed, convenience, and security in one payment account.

02 / Increase Wallet Loads

Wallet loads were one of the clearest signals of whether people were accepting the product as a place to keep and use money. It was not just about a one-time load amount; we also wanted to influence how often users loaded money, whether they came back for recurring loads, and how much they were comfortable loading each time. As Zapp Account became useful across more payment contexts, the load behavior became a stronger indicator of whether people trusted it for everyday use.

03 / Elevate The Overall Experience

The experience also needed to do a better job of selling and explaining the product inside PayZapp. The rebrand gave us room to refresh the visual language, make the landing page more load-centric, and improve how important features like auto load showed up. We also wanted clearer communication through nudges and progress states, so users could understand what Zapp Account offered, what they had already unlocked, and what they could do next. These were not just business targets; they came from our discussions with leadership about what Zapp Account needed to become.

How did we approach it?

Some competitor research, design with independent rollouts in mind, and study what already worked.

We translated the goals from the previous section into a planning structure. The work had to improve adoption, load amount, load frequency, and the overall account experience, but the first step was deciding what each part of the experience needed to communicate.

Video KYC was actually rolled out first.

Tackling Video KYC And Visual Refresh as two separate threads

Video KYC and the visual refresh were being handled by two different engineering teams, so the approach needed to support either one going live first without blocking the other. The bigger reason was cohesion: if Video KYC launched before the Zapp Account rebrand, it still had to feel like a natural part of the existing PayZapp Wallet proposition. And if the visual refresh moved first, it had to make room for Video KYC without depending on that flow being live yet. That meant the design language, onboarding logic, and account communication could not depend on a single combined release.

Study What Already Worked

The data suggested that people who used PayZapp Wallet mostly used it for bills and recharges, because those were the easiest and most prominent ways to use it. Some users also used auto-load, but it was not very evident in the experience. That mattered because auto-load could drive more convenient usage and higher or more frequent load amounts, both of which were important goals for Zapp Account.

We also spoke to a handful of people who used the wallet to understand what they expected from a wallet-like payments product.

Swipe to Pay, offered by Zeta, is a quicker and safer way of authorizing payments using a single swipe. It's bound to the SIM.

Niche features like Swipe to Pay and bank transfers were used but poorly known — people had stumbled into them rather than discovered them intentionally.

For most users, the wallet was primarily a CashPoint conversion tool — cashpoints earned on offers were added as wallet balance and spent on recharges and bill payments.

Auto-load was largely unknown, but the problem it solves — running out of balance mid-payment — was something people had experienced and found frustrating.

The following points became the working conclusions from what we found.

01 / Choose The Right Value Prop For Entry Points

For adoption, the first question was how we could sell Zapp Account wherever users encountered it inside PayZapp. The entry points could not simply say "upgrade" or "complete KYC"; they had to make the account feel worth the effort. The pitch needed to be built around these three values.

Fast

Speed could be communicated through quicker payment methods: tap and pay, scan-and-pay UPI, and the ability to move through supported payment modes with less friction.

Secure

Security could lean on the trust of PayZapp as an HDFC Bank app, with the compliance expectations and safeguards that came with that context.

Convenient

Convenience could come from bringing payment modes and controls into one place, so users could manage Zapp Account payment settings, enable or disable payment methods, and move across supported ways to pay with less effort.

02 / Make Video KYC Clear And Trackable

Video KYC could easily become a drop-off-heavy journey because it involved many steps. Users would need to share personal and PAN details, pay an applicable upgrade fee, prepare documents, ensure proper lighting, maintain a stable internet connection, complete the video verification, wait for the verification to go through, and then receive the upgraded Zapp Account. The approach had to make this complex process feel simple for people enrolling into it: explain what would be required, show progress clearly, and make the next step feel trackable instead of uncertain.

03 / Make The First Screen More Load-Centric

The older PayZapp Wallet landing page did not really push loading money as the hero action. It focused more heavily on the virtual card, which made sense for the older wallet framing but not for the positioning Zapp Account was trying to take. A load-centric screen would need to make it obvious where to add money, and make auto-load easier to understand: when a user's account balance fell below a set amount, money could be added automatically so the payment could go through. We saw room to improve the information architecture of the first screen so loading money, repeat usage, and account capability could become clearer from the first visit.

04 / Organize The Experience Around Contextual Levers

The homepage could become more than an entry point. It could act as an intuitive control surface for the account, while also quietly advertising the most useful reasons to come back and use it.

Place Auto-Load Where It Made Sense

Auto-load directly supported recurring loads, so it needed to be visible, transparent, and placed close to the account balance and load actions.

Make Offers Clear And Visible

Offers and CashPoints could help users understand the value of Zapp Account, especially because one CashPoint equaled one rupee.

Expose Payment Settings

Payment cards, payment settings, and enable-or-disable controls needed to be easy to find instead of feeling buried inside the account.

Let The Homepage Explain Itself

The first screen could work as a self-explaining surface, showing what the account could do and guiding users toward the next useful action.

Use State-Based Rich Nudges

Rich nudges could provide contextual education across different account states, helping users understand what Zapp Account was, how it worked, what they had unlocked, and what action could move them forward.

Prototyped the load interaction using Principle to really nail the motion feel.

How did we solve it?

We made the first screen more load-centric, clarified the Video KYC journey, and created reusable patterns that could support the Zapp Account system inside PayZapp.

01 / First fold

Make Loading Money The Primary Action

We reorganized the landing page so the balance, load money action, auto-load, and key account actions were easier to notice from the first fold. The screen had to make the account feel useful immediately, not just decorative.

02 / KYC clarity

Making KYC Transparent

We made the upgrade journey more explicit by showing what users needed to do, where they were in the process, and what capabilities they would unlock after completing Video KYC.

What were the results?

+42.5% in load amounts, +30% in adoption, and a steady lift in feature exploration.

The revised experience helped make Zapp Account easier to understand, easier to enter, and easier to use as a place to load and spend money inside PayZapp.

What's Next?

Turning the Zapp Account into a full money dashboard inside the PayZapp ecosystem.

Phase 1 was foundational. It successfully migrated the user base to the new mental model of the Zapp Account.

Version 2 (currently in progress) builds on this by evolving the Zapp Account into a full dashboard view. This will integrate the account even deeper into the broader PayZapp ecosystem, creating a seamless, interconnected financial hub for the user.
